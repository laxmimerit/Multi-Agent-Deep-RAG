### Focused Query 1: Apple capital return program (dividends and buybacks) 2023 and 2024
Apple continued its aggressive capital return program through both share repurchases (buybacks) and dividends, significantly increasing its buyback activity in 2024.

*   **Share Repurchases (Buybacks):**
    *   **FY 2024:** Apple repurchased 499 million shares of its common stock for a total of **$95.0 billion** (cash flow statement shows $94.95 billion). 
    *   **FY 2023:** Apple repurchased 471 million shares for a total of **$77.55 billion**.
    *   **Significant Authorization:** On May 2, 2024, the Board of Directors authorized an additional **$110 billion** for share repurchases, one of the largest in corporate history.
*   **Dividends:**
    *   **FY 2024:** Total cash dividends paid were **$15.23 billion**. The quarterly dividend was raised in May 2024 from $0.24 to **$0.25 per share**, resulting in a total of $0.98 declared per share for the fiscal year.
    *   **FY 2023:** Total cash dividends paid were **$15.03 billion**. The total dividend declared per share was $0.94.

### Focused Query 2: Apple cash flow status (Operating, Investing, Financing) FY2023 and FY2024
Apple's cash flow remains robust, allowing it to fund its massive capital return program primarily through cash generated from operations.

*   **Operating Cash Flow:**
    *   **FY 2024:** $118.25 billion (an increase from $110.54 billion in 2023).
    *   **FY 2023:** $110.54 billion.
*   **Investing Cash Flow:**
    *   **FY 2024:** Generated $2.94 billion (driven by proceeds from maturities and sales of marketable securities of $62.3 billion, offset by $48.7 billion in purchases and $9.4 billion in CapEx).
    *   **FY 2023:** Generated $3.71 billion.
*   **Financing Cash Flow:**
    *   **FY 2024:** Used $121.98 billion (primarily for the $95 billion in buybacks and $15.2 billion in dividends).
    *   **FY 2023:** Used $108.49 billion.

### Summary
Apple's financial strategy in 2023 and 2024 emphasized returning nearly all free cash flow to shareholders. In 2024, the company significantly accelerated its share buybacks to $95 billion, supported by a record $110 billion authorization in May. Despite a slight dip in net income in 2024 ($93.7B vs $97.0B in 2023), operating cash flow grew by approximately 7%, demonstrating the efficiency of Apple's business model. The company continues to maintain a "net cash neutral" goal, progressively reducing its cash surplus by returning it to investors.
