**Data Trends and Key Insights:**

Let's analyze the trends for each expense category and the overall total:

*   **Research & Development (R&D):**
    *   **Trend:** Began at 30% in Q2'22, peaked at 33% in Q3'22 and Q1'23, and then showed a gradual but consistent decline. It was 29% in Q2'23, 27% in Q3'23, 26% in Q4'23 (its lowest point), and has settled at 27% in Q1'24 and Q2'24.
    *   **Insight:** R&D consistently represents the largest single expense category. While it saw a reduction from its peak of 33% to 27% by Q3'23, it remains a substantial investment for Meta.

*   **Marketing & Sales (M&S):**
    *   **Trend:** Started at 12% in Q2'22, peaked at 14% in Q3'22 and Q4'22. It then showed a significant and steady decline: 11% in Q1'23, 10% in Q2'23, 8% in Q3'23 and Q4'23, and further dropped to 7% in Q1'24 and Q2'24.
    *   **Insight:** This category saw one of the most significant relative reductions, nearly halving its percentage of revenue from its peak of 14% to 7% over the two-year period, suggesting a strong focus on optimizing marketing and sales efficiency.

*   **General & Administrative (G&A):**
    *   **Trend:** Started at 10% in Q2'22, fluctuated between 10% and 12% in 2022, and peaked at 13% in Q2'23. Following this, it experienced a dramatic drop to 6% in Q3'23 and Q4'23, before rising slightly to 9% in Q1'24 and Q2'24.
    *   **Insight:** This category also saw a very sharp reduction, falling by more than half (from 13% to 6%) in a single quarter (Q2'23 to Q3'23). This drastic change, along with the M&S reduction, points to a company-wide cost-cutting initiative or significant operational restructuring in mid-2023.
